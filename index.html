Work

I’m a 5th-year student at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research- Thiruvananthapuram, doing my Integrated BS-MS course, majoring in Physics and minoring in Maths. I am now actively looking for PhD opportunities in the fields of Many-Body Physics, Open-Quantum Systems, Strongly Correlated Systems, Quantum Information and Technologies and quantum Condensed Matter Theory.

In the context of my master's project thesis, I am working with Prof. Anil Shaji. We aim to explore the transport properties of electrons in Quantum Dot Arrays. Considering an open system of Quantum Dots, we study different characteristic behavior of electrons in both classical and quantum regimes. The project could lead to advancements in quantum computing technologies, where controlling and manipulating electrons is essential for building quantum circuits.

I’m also working with Dr. Shovan Dutta at Raman Research Institute, Bangalore, to study how the frustration caused by the topology of a network can affect the groundstate properties of an antiferromagnetic lattice. We explore complex networks and their graph properties that influence the lattice behaviors. Considering spins seated at the nodes of a general complex network, we mainly focus on studying the ground-state net magnetization of the lattice.

Thanks to the pandemic, I got plenty of time to do a reading project guided by Prof. Anil Shaji in 2021. I studied the paper , titled "Transport through Quantum Dots: An intro via master equation simulations" written by RA Bush et.al. and reproduced the results published. With the help of my advisor, I also obtained the results for a system of double quantum dots, writing the master equation inspired from the info given in the paper.It helped me get a deep knowledge of Quantum Transport and understand the physics involved with Quantum dots.
